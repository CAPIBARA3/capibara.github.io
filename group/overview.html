Joan Alcaide-Núñez

×

I lead the CAPIBARA mission, managing project planning, progress monitoring, and our relationship with PLD Space’s SPARK program. My role focuses on coordinating the team and ensuring effective collaboration.

My research in Cosmology explores the Universe’s origin, evolution, and fate, with a focus on using GRBs (gamma-ray bursts) as standard candles to measure distances to galaxies. Previous work include using Cepheids (2023) and Ia Supernovae (2024) as distance indicators to compute the Hubble constant and other cosmological parameters.

I’ve demonstrated research excellence through the Youth and Science program by Fundació Catalunya La Pedrera, a research stay at ICE-CSIC on Supernovae Cosmology, and an internship at DLR, where I analyzed thermal images.

Committed to outreach, I participated in the CosmoCaixa Explainers program, tutor STEM subjects weekly, and mentor aspiring STEM students. My Genetics10 project, an open-source tool, makes genetics accessible through a Jupyter Notebook and API, supporting multiple languages for schools worldwide.

Driven by a passion for science, technology, and community, I aim to inspire others through teaching, research, and innovative projects.

I'm currently studying high school in Spain (2º Bachillerato) and I would like to start the physics career next year in order to become an astrophysics investigator. Before that, I have had the great opportunity to be involved in CAPIBARA's project, in which I am taking part in the scientific team. Personally, I'm a hard-working person who has a big interest and desire to enhance my knowledge. Recently, I have been studying stellar astrophysics and solar system objects. Other interests include chemistry, astrophotography, politics, philosophy and agriculture.
