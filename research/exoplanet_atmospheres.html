Exoplanet Atmospheres

Research Initiative - CAPIBARA Project

Introduction

Since the discovery of exoplanets in the 1990s, the study of these distant worlds has evolved rapidly, with new methods revealing atmospheric properties. Our research explores how cosmic ray interactions with exoplanetary atmospheres might produce detectable high-energy emissions, using tools in X-ray and gamma-ray astronomy.

Research Focus

This study aims to detect secondary cosmic ray (SCR) signatures in exoplanet atmospheres. We hypothesize that cosmic ray interactions create particle showers detectable as high-energy emissions, offering a way to analyze exoplanetary atmospheric properties.

Methods

We use transit observations alongside X-ray and gamma-ray photometry and spectroscopy to observe possible SCR emissions in selected exoplanet systems, applying space-based data to maximize sensitivity to high-energy interactions.
