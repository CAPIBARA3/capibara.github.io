GRBs for Cosmological Distances

Research Initiative - CAPIBARA Project

Introduction to GRBs and Their Importance in Cosmology

The Hubble constant is the expansion rate of the Universe and a key value for cosmology, the study of the Universe. However, there is a diverging discrepancy between different methods to compute this value.

Gamma-Ray Bursts (GRBs) are the most energetic explosions in the Universe, and they can be observed at great cosmological distances. In just a few seconds, GRBs release more energy than the Sun emits over its entire lifetime.

We aim to standardize GRBs as distance indicators, expanding the method of the local distance ladder and providing new tools for cosmology. Not only will this help refine the Hubble constant, but it will also allow for more precise fitting of cosmological models, leading to a better understanding of other fundamental parameters of the Universe.

Our Research Focus

Our research focuses on leveraging GRBs as "cosmic beacons" to measure cosmological distances and better understand the evolution of the Universe. We aim to introduce a further standard candle to the distance ladder method, which is capable of reaching much greater distances.
